# Topographic / abstract glowing-line

**Use when:** the user wants something less photographic and more graphic-design-y.
Best for evergreen banners that need to age well, or when no specific
campaign is active.

**Composition rules:**
- 4:1 aspect ratio
- Pure abstract — no recognisable objects
- Brand palette: lime + violet on near-black
- Lines or topography suggesting infrastructure / data flow
- Generous low-density area where text and logo land

## Prompt to paste into OpenAI Images 2.0

> Wide ultra-thin 4:1 abstract banner image (1584 × 396), dark charcoal background (#0E0F10 transitioning to #15171A), faint glowing fiber-optic strands radiating diagonally from the lower-left corner, lime green (#9AF23C) and violet (#A78BFA) bioluminescent threads on near-black, very low information density on the right two-thirds, subtle film grain, no text, no logos, cinematic minimal aesthetic, contemplative not flashy.

## Variants to try

For a topographic-map feel:

> ...replace fiber strands with concentric topographic contour lines, lime on the high-elevation lines and violet on the lows, faint glow at line intersections, generous quiet area on the right...

For a circuit-board macro:

> ...extreme macro of a circuit board surface, traces glowing faintly lime and violet, shallow depth of field with most of the right side falling out of focus into pure dark, near-black background...

For a starfield / constellation feel:

> ...sparse constellation of small glowing points connected by thin lines, lime and violet pinpoints on deep charcoal, like a network topology diagram rendered as nightscape, generous empty space across the right side...
